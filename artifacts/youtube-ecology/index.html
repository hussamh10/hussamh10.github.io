Thesis under review: YouTube's affordances construct rather than merely reflect user preferences. Changing an affordance — how engagement is operationalized (CTR→watch-time), personalization intensity, monetization, metric visibility — changes what content is produced and consumed. Documented affordance changes are treated as natural experiments; against the "we give people what they want" defense and the Munger–Phillips supply-and-demand account.
