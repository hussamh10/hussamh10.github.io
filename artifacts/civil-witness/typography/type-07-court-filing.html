§ 1 — Subject

This dispatch concerns the appearance of the phrase "anti-state elements seeking to destabilize the federation" on six Pakistani prime-time news broadcasts within a twenty-four hour period.

 

§ 2 — Origin

On 12 April 2026 at 16:30 PKT, a senior official used the phrase, extemporaneously, in a televised press conference. The phrase did not appear in any prepared briefing released to journalists.

 

§ 3 — Subsequent broadcast

Between 17:14 and 20:42 the same evening, the phrase appeared in chyrons on six independent channels, in three cities, with no rewriting or paraphrase. Five of six occurrences fell within a four-hour window. Four of six were placed in the lower-third chyron rather than anchor read.

 

§ 4 — Finding

The structural pattern — verbatim wording, clustered timing, identical broadcast position — is inconsistent with independent newsroom selection. We do not claim coordination on the basis of a single phrase. We claim this is the kind of artifact that, repeated across cases, draws the architecture of manufactured consent.

 

§ 5 — On the record

The full archive of broadcast captures, with timestamps and channel metadata, is filed under Sijill 04/127 and is available on request. Civil Witness is keeping the record.
