The 24-Hour Talking Point.six channels.

How a single phrase about "anti-state elements" appeared on six Pakistani TV channels within 24 hours of a press conference — same words, same order, same emphasis.

On April 12 at 16:30, a senior official used the phrase "anti-state elements seeking to destabilize the federation" in a press conference. By April 13 at 16:30 — exactly twenty-four hours later — that phrase had appeared, verbatim, in chyrons on six different prime-time news broadcasts.

From the field

This is what coordinated talking points look like in the data. The phrase did not emerge across newsrooms organically over a working day. It appeared, in the same words, in clustered time, in the same broadcast position, in six places at once.

We do not claim coordination on the basis of a single phrase. We claim that the structural pattern — same words, same hour, same chyron slot — is the kind of artifact that, repeated across cases, draws the architecture of manufactured consent.
