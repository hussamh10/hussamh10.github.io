II

Dilluns · Day Two

The mountain, the tram, the bay.

Stay put in Sóller. The Tramuntana is at your door — walk the famous dry-stone gorge path in the morning, ride the 1913 wooden tram down to swim, climb up for sunset, dinner back in town. Almost no driving.

  1. until 09:45

    01Plaça de Sa Constitució — slow start

    Coffee + ensaïmada under the plane trees in the main square. Look up at Sant Bartomeu — the modernist façade is by Joan Rubió, a Gaudí disciple, 1904. The wooden train from Palma rolls in around 10:00; watch it arrive, then walk.

    Open in Maps Sa Granja (café)
  2. ≈15 min · 1.2 km on foot through orange groves to Biniaraix hamlet
    10:00 — 13:00

    02Camí des Barranc de Biniaraix — the hike

    Walk from Sóller centre to Biniaraix (a tiny stone hamlet, 200 souls). From the village square, the Camí enters the gorge — UNESCO-listed dry-stone trail (Camí de Pedra en Sec). Restored medieval steps zig-zag up through olive terraces and holm-oak shade, drystone walls everywhere. You set the turnaround. ≈1hr up gets you to a flat threshing floor (l'Era) — eat the orange you brought, sit, listen. Walk back down. Total ~2.5–3hr easy pace. Never steep, always shaded. The essence of the Sierra.

    Trailhead in Maps Biniaraix village
    Bring 1.5L water each, an orange or two from the market, a hat. Trail shoes ideal but sturdy trainers fine. No need to summit Es Cornadors — the gorge itself is the gift.

  5. 16:00 — 18:30

    05Platja d'en Repic — swim + lounge

    The quieter cove on the west side of the port (skip the busier Platja del Port nearer the tram stop). Sandy, families, beach bar with vermut + olives. Calm water, perfect for an afternoon you don't want to end. Or rent a kayak/SUP for an hour to paddle the bay — the cliffs around the port are best from the water.

    Open in Maps
    Optional swap If you'd rather one big show-stopper: the Tramontana Cruise boat from Port de Sóller goes to Sa Calobra and the mouth of the Torrent de Pareis gorge. Departs ~10:15, returns ~16:30. Skips the hike but is one of the most jaw-dropping coast rides in Spain.

Carta · Tick as you taste

Things you must put in your mouth.

Budget-friendly local finds, with a few splurges marked. Look for a celler — old wine-cellar restaurants, low ceilings, huge wooden barrels. Skip anywhere with a multilingual photo menu out front.

Breakfast

Esmorzar — slow, sweet, short

Lunch

Dinar — the meal that matters

Dinner

Take home

Glass in hand

Hierbas mallorquinas

Anise/herb digestif, served ice-cold after meals. Often free pour at cellers — say yes.

Palo

Bittersweet carob liqueur with soda. Acquired taste, very local. Try once.

Vi de la terra

House wine from a Mallorcan jug — €2–3 a glass at any celler.
